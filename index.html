Mona Lisa

The "Mona Lisa," painted by Leonardo da Vinci between 1503 and 1506, is one of the most iconic and recognized artworks in the world. This masterpiece is housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris. The painting is renowned for its enigmatic smile, subtle use of sfumato (a technique to blend colors and create a blurred effect), and da Vinci's meticulous attention to detail. The subject of the portrait is believed to be Lisa Gherardini, a woman from Florence. The Mona Lisa's fame extends beyond its artistic merits; it has become a symbol of art and culture, attracting millions of visitors to the Louvre each year.
